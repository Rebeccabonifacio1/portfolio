UX Design & Site Building


Out of all my passions, UX Design and Site Building is my favorite creative pursuit. I feel unbelievably fulfilled when I overcome a challenging code problem or design a site that showcases a specific interest or hobby. Upon graduating from James Madison University in May of 2021, I intend to continue to pursue my interest in Web Design and attend a Masters program for Interaction Design. I am looking forward to the continuation of this study and learning more about the ins and outs of Web Design.


In addition to the three sites linked below that I coded and designed over the last year, I also built this portfolio as a final project for SMAD 308: Interactive Design II. Please feel free to navigate through my most recent work below!


Astrology Bootstraps Site

My Astrology Bootstraps Site, completed in November of 2020, uses the Responsive Bootstraps Platform in addition to HTML and Custom CSS. The site is aimed at educating and informing its visitors on the subject of astrology, a concept that I am well-versed in and have strong ties to. The site is set up to promote user engagement, working to explain astrology and why a visitor should believe in it, then providing basic info on the three main concepts of the craft, signs, planets, and houses. Each section is laid out in a similar format so that the user knows what to expect and can easily scroll through the site. Click below to see the site!

Co-Star Astrology Redesign

My App Redesign Site, completed in September of 2020, uses mainly HTML and CSS to provide an in-depth app flow redesign for the popular app, Co-Star Astrology. The website is based on user-research I conducted, which gave me the necessary information to come up with 3 major pain points for the app. This user research helped me design a new wireframe for the app, as well as developing suggestions for how the app can better fit user needs. This site focuses mostly on the concept of user research in relation to UX Design and helped me understand the importance of designing with your user in mind. Click below to see the site!

Arati Kreibich for Congress Site

My first Responsive Bootstraps Site, designed in April of 2020 for a congressional candidate running in my district, uses the Responsive Bootstraps Platform in addition to HTML and Custom CSS. This redesign worked to provide the necessary information for Kreibich’s campaign in a more user-friendly way such as a biography, endorsements, issues, and volunteer information. This site was my first experience with Bootstraps and my first well-developed website in general. While I have improved in skill and tact since this site, I appreciate looking back and seeing how much I’ve grown along the way. Click below to see the site!
