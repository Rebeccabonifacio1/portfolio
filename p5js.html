P5.JS Experience

P5.js is a javascript library for more creative types of coding. This language focuses primarily on art and illustrations and has been a wonderful learning experience for me outside of typical UX Design. Below you will find some of the sketches I worked on this past semester in SMAD 308: Interactive Design II. You can easily navigate between the finished product and the code on this web page, or feel free to check out my open processing page to see more of my work in P5.js!


Moon Clock



The above sketch, completed in October 2020, uses p5.js to show time. I attempted to model this sketch after the moon, taking a unique spin on the assignment and using my creative liberty. My moon clock keeps accurate time based off of your computer or phone's internal system and was a great deal of fun to code!

Spotify Genre Data Visualization


This sketch, completed in October 2020, uses p5.js to share data. I took my top 50 listened to musical artists from 2020 and broke them into genres. I then visually shared this data in a bar graph. Each time you refresh the sketch, the bars and corresponding genre names will change color! I also added in some music notes and record players to add to the creativity of the design!

Self Portrait


The above sketch, completed in October 2020, is a self portrait designed with code. This was a lot of fun to do, and while I had less creative liberty with this sketch than others, I added in the galaxy background to show off my interests. The stars in the galaxy background will move with your mouse, give it a try!

Katmai National Park Bears


The above sketch, completed in September of 2020, was done in honor of my roommate and best friend, who absolutely loves Katmai National Park and their annual fat bear week. The bear will follow your mouse while the fish swim across the page!

Abstract Painting


The above sketch, completed in September of 2020, was my first sketch and real experience within P5.js. This assignment asked us to recreate an abstract painting by Piet Mondrian, of which I chose his Composition piece. This was a wonderful learning experience and helped me quickly understand the creative facilities of p5.js.
