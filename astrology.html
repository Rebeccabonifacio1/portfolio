The Basics of Astrology

Get Started


What is Astrology?

Astrology is the idea that the stars and planetary positions play a role in human affairs and events. Astrology has been dated as far back to the 2nd millennium BCE and was a core component of ancient civilizations and cultures. Astrology argues that the placement of the planet’s positions during one’s time of birth plays a large role in that individual’s character and potential achievements or struggles in their life. Beyond this, one’s birth chart can interact with present day placements to help explain challenges that an individual or the world could be going through.


Ultimately, people use astrology for one of the following reasons:


Astrology is built off of 12 signs that each represent a 20 day birth cycle. These signs materilize differently for each individual, but can help to explain certain tendencies and personality traits. These signs are located in both planets and houses. Each sign is located in one of the eight planets in our solar system and one of twelve houses. Both planets and houses have different meanings and characteristics behind them, which can tie into understanding a person or situation in relation to their sign’s location in one of these placements.



Astrology is a relatively complicated subject that often times gets boiled down to a pseudoscience where people tend to evade responsibility for their negative characteristics. In actuality, when you take the time to understand astrology in relation to all of the aspects listed above, one will often find that the concept resonates.



Why Should I believe it?

Astrology is a personal journey, therefore, the best way to truly experience it is to do your own research. However, if you’re not ready to dive in yet, it would be best to look at how astrology has predicted past events. Astrology has the power to answer a number of questions about yourself and the world, you can get assurance about the future, learn about your current situation, future decisions, and feel connected to the universe thanks to astrology.



As mentioned above, astrology has been an important part of culture, dating as far back as BC. Astrology has been used by historical figures, such as kings and popes, and previous presidents of the United States like Benjamin Franklin and Ronald Reagan. There is a reason this practice has remained instilled in society; it provides accurate insights on people’s personas, the way in which they interact with one another, and can help predict world events. Hearing about another person’s experience with the craft may not be enough to convince you to take the dive into astrology. Everyone’s experience with astrology is different, but when you take the time to truly learn about it, you may be surprised by its accuracy.



Where do I begin?

Signs

When people think of astrology, they typically think of their sun sign. Sun signs are one of 12 zodiacs that get assigned to an individual depending on when they were born. The first sign of the zodiac, Aries, begins on March 20 and goes until April 19. This reoccurring monthly cycle repeats throughout the year, with Pisces being the last sign of the zodiac, staring on February 18 and going until March 19. While your sun sign is an important part of your persona, everyone is made up of more than just this one placement. Upon calculating your birth chart, you’ll discover that you have a sign for every planetary placement, as well as a rising sign, or an ascendant, which changes depending on your birth time.


While each sign represents different ideals and personality traits, signs are grouped together based on the elements. Fire signs, Aries, Leo and Sagittarius, are known for being passionate, exuberant and feisty. Earth signs, Taurus, Virgo and Capricorn are typically grounded, practical and hard-working. Air Signs, Gemini, Libra and Aquarius, are unique, intellectual and curious. Finally, water signs, Cancer, Scorpio, and Pisces, are known for being intuitive, emotional and dreamy. The more elemental placements you have in your chart, the more likely it is for this element to dictate your persona, same as having a lot of placements in one specific sign.


Signs are the most intricate aspect of astrology, so it’s okay if you don’t relate to all the traits and characteristics people with these sign placements typically hold. Below you will find a brief description of the makeup of each zodiac sign, starting with Aries. This is merely an introduction and it’s highly encouraged to do some more extensive research off of your specific sign placements if you’d like to learn more.

Planets

Just as important as signs, is the concept of planets. The makeup of a birth chart is dependent on planetary positions - each of us have one of the above 12 signs in every planet. Signs stay in certain planets for years, such as Pluto, that changes every 10 years, or can change as quickly as every two days like the moon. In addition to the 8 planets in our solar system and the sun and the moon, every person has a rising sign. Your rising sign, or ascendant, changes every two hours and is essentially the makeup of your being, often times defining how others perceive you. Although your rising sign is not based off of a planet, it holds the same weight as the other planetary positions, making it just as vastly important for understanding astrology.


Similarly to the signs listed above, the planets represent far more than listed below. Consider the below statements a broad overview of how these planets may affect your persona based on your birth chart. Remember, the best way to get a true understanding of the planets in relation to you is to learn what sign is in each planet on your chart. It is through the characteristics of both the sign and the planet that you will discover the most about yourself

Sun

The sun governs ego and self. The sun is the basis of our personality, it is where the truth of our nature exists and where our identities are forged. This placement is based off when you were born and typically changes every 25-30 days. Often times, when someone asks you what your sign is, they’re actually asking “what’s your sun sign?” You are who you are because of your sun sign and it’s important to recognize the impact this placement plays. Your sun sign may not resonate with you if you don’t have other planetary placements in the same same or perhaps are strongly ruled by another sign. Regardless, the sun sign dictates a core piece of your persona and is worth understanding.

Moon

The moon governs emotion and feeling. Whereas your sun sign represents your outward personality, your moon sign focuses inward and dictates your emotional core. It determines how you process feelings and emotions and helps paint a much larger picture of who you are as an individual, drawing connections to your childhood as well. The moon can also be linked to your relationship with female figures. Moon placement changes every two and a half days, making your moon sign a much specific placement. The moon symbolizes unconsciousness, emotions, instincts, habits and moods, and can help you better understand the inner workings of yourself.

Rising

Your rising sign, or ascendant, is not a planet, but plays an integral role in your persona just the same. This placement represents the way others see you, your general impression and your spontaneous reactions. Upon meeting someone for the first time, often times you will be interacting with their rising sign. While it may be difficult for you to resonate with your rising sign, it is a very outward and important position. Ascendants change every two hours,so it’s extremely important that you know your exact birth time when calculating this placement. This is a great position for understanding how we interact with the world and how we are perceived.


Mercury

Mercury governs transmitting information and communication with others. Your intelligence resides within your mercury sign and helps you communicate, explain, and understand. This manifests physically as listening, reading, writing, and talking, so understanding the components of your mercury sign can help you communicate and learn in the best way possible. Mercury placement changes every 3-4 weeks, so it is typically the same as your sun sign, but if you were born towards the beginning or end of a cycle it may not be! Mercury symbolizes mind, communication, intellect, reason, language and intelligence.

Venus

Venus governs love and beauty. Our Venus sign determines how we give and receive love, and it’s where we find comfort and support within ourselves and others. Venus is a very dreamy placement that rules all aesthetically pleasing things in life, such as your material possessions or where you find pleasure. By understanding your Venus sign, you are opening yourself up to fulfilling relationships because you’re able to articulate what you need and understand what your loved ones expect in return. Venus placements change every 4-5 weeks, and this planet ultimately symbolizes love, attraction, relationship, art, beauty and harmony.

Earth

Since we are creatures of Earth, the planet does not hold an important role in your birth chart the same way other planets do. We are positioned on Earth when we are born, and because your birth chart serves as a snapshot of the sky when you were born, Earth is not included in that snapshot. As en element, Earth manifests as a sense of duty, responsibility, and reliability. It rules the logical mind, and the majority of dependable and practical signs are Earth-based. While Earth may not hold a lot of weight in our specific birth chart, it’s important to recognize the qualities our planet represents.



Mars

Mars governs will and courage. Its a fiery and passionate planet that helps us understand how we assert ourselves - think of it as the fight in the fight or flight battle. This planet helps us understand our aggression and volatility, but at the same time, our bravery and self-empowerment. If you’re feeling stuck or passive, it most often is a result of your Mars sign. Mars placements change every 6-7 weeks and ultimately symbolizes aggression, sex, action, desire, competition and courage.

Jupiter

Jupiter governs abundance and generosity. This planet fuels all sort of optimism in our lives, and is considered a planet of miracle and luck. This placement helps you grow, experiencing new things in every possible capacity. It shows us how we will find happiness and purpose as a person, as well as where in life we may be the most lucky. Jupiter placements change every 12-13 months and ultimately symbolizes luck, growth, expansion, optimism, abundance and understanding.

Saturn

Saturn governs discipline and commitment. You can think of Saturn as the solar system’s task master. Saturn will often bring difficult challenges to light, but only for your own good.Your Saturn Return, which occurs in your 29th year of life, can cause a lot of hardship and perhaps even be classified as a quarter-life crisis, but only does so to help you become a better person. Saturn placements change every 2-3 years and ultimately symbolizes structure, law, restriction, discipline and obligation.



Uranus

Uranus governs individuality and change. Uranus is an outer planet, meaning it takes seven years to orbit the sun and change sign positions. Because of this, Uranus is more likely to govern a group of people and speaks largely to the core goals of a generation, rather than individuals. Uranus is a strong planet and ultimately symbolizes eccentricity, unpredictable changes, rebellion and reformation.

Neptune

Neptune governs intuition and healing. Neptune helps you step away from your ego and self-awareness and exist in the in-between. This planet helps you develop your spiritual awareness and focus on the difference of ego and self, in whatever way this manifests for you. Neptune placements change every 10-12 years, ruling a generation more than an individual and ultimately symbolizes dreams, intuition, mysticism, and delusion.

Pluto

Pluto governs transformation and power. Even though Pluto’s planetary distinction is up for debate, it holds a lot of weight in astrological influence. Pluto rules over our destinies, giving life to our biggest desires, while simultaneously reminding us how short life is. This placement encourages you to live life to the fullest! Pluto placements change every 12-15 years, and ultimately symbolizes transformation, power, death, and evolution.



Houses

The last vital piece of information for understanding astrology is house placements. Houses, listed from House 1, which is always placed in your rising sign, to House 12, each rule specific characteristics and traits similarly to planets and signs. The position of a house will help you understand how a specific sign or planetary placement will manifest in your life and where it’s most likely for problems to arise in relation to these characteristics.


When a planet visits a house, they light up that part of your chart and energize the house’s traits. Astrologers often use the houses to predict which parts of your life will come into focus, and where you can take the best possible action. While it’s important to understand the basics of each house, it may be the easiest to comprehend by looking at your specific birth chart and which houses hold the most weight. Lastly, while every person has a sign in every house, they don’t all hold the same weight, so depending on which birth chart calculator you use, you may not see all the houses on your chart. This just means that that specific house/sign does not hold much weight over your persona and life path, but it is still vital to understanding the ins and outs of yourself.


Below you will find basic descriptors of each house to help you get accustomed with the concept. As with all parts of astrology, these are merely summaries, and it is likely that these can manifest differently in your life. If you’re interested in learning more, it’s recommended to look up the house in relation to your specific sign and planetary placements to best understand how they interact.


The first house begins the zodiac, and therefore covers all the firsts, be that first impressions, the self and appearance, leadership, new initiatives, fresh starts and beginnings. No matter who you are, the sign placed in your first house will always be your rising sign or ascendant, as this is the sign that rules outer appearances and firsts. At it’s core, the first house is a great indicator of someone’s attitude, identity, and approach to life.

The second house covers all matters related to your immediate material and physical environment, think of the five senses - taste, smells, sound, touch and sights. The second house is also a great indicator of income, money and perhaps the more material parts of life. At it’s core, the second house is a great indicator of someone’s self esteem, daily routines, values, possessions, priorities, habits, and work ethic.

The third house rules all forms of communication, from talking and thinking to more technological based communication like cell phones and instant messaging. This house is very important! In addition, the third house covers the more emotional and personal sides of life, like siblings, community and childhood. At it’s core, the third house is a great indicator of someone’s mind, social activity, and early education.



The fourth house sits at the bottom of the zodiac wheel and rules the foundational parts of life. This manifests as your house, privacy, basic security, parent relationships, and nurturing. This house helps us understand where we find comfort and security in life. At it’s core, the fourth house is a great indicator of someone’s own parental abilities, how they practice self-care and tune into emotions and familial ties.

The fifth house governs self-expression and creativity. This house helps us become one with our inner child, bringing about the enthusiasm and love that most children feel for life. It also rules fertility and romantic relationships. At it’s core, the fifth house is a great indicator of how we love and play with others, where we would succeed most in creative pursuits, and describes our spirit and joy towards life.

The sixth house is the domain of health and services. This house often manifests as the more routine and mundane things in life - schedules, routines, organization. The 6th house is also directly connected to physical activity and how we live a natural and healthy lifestyle. At it’s core, the sixth house is a great indicator of health, systems, analytical nature, work habits, sense of useful, and our service to others.



The seventh house is the sector of our relationships, both with ourselves and others. This house governs all partnerships and relationship-associated matters, like business deals and marriage. This house can also help you understand where your hardest dependencies in relationships might exist. At it’s core, the seventh house is a great indicator of relationships, equality, and sharing.

The eighth house is the most mysterious of the zodiac, and is typically considered the house of transformation and death. This house rules the more occult and mystic things in life and if you have a lot of signs here you might be deeply vested in this part of life. It also rules other people's property and money.At it’s core, the eighth house is a great indicator of intimacy, assets, inheritance and joint ventures/goals.

The ninth house covers higher mind, expansion and travel, in the material and in your mind. It is known as the house of paradigms and represents your journey through life, both literally and metaphorically. This house can help you understand what things may surprise or inspire you. At it’s core, the ninth house is a great indicator of inspiration, education, luck, risk, philosophy, adventure, morals and ethics.



The tenth house is the most public part of the chart, governing structures, corporations, tradition and public image. The sign in your tenth house is also called the midheaven and can be a good indicator of your career path or work-related goals. At it’s core, the tenth house is a great indicator of long-term goals, structure, status, reputation, masculinity, experts and fame.

The eleventh house governs teams, friendships, groups and society. It is the house of friends and future, and can help explain how the idea of society may change in a lifetime. This house says a lot about a generation. This house also rules electronic media, social justice and humanitarian affairs. At it’s core, the eleventh house is a great indicator of social awareness, hopes, wishes, and the future.

The twelfth house is the last of the zodiac, therefore it’s no surprise that it rules endings. This house covers the final stages of a project, completion and surrender. Materialistically, it’s associated with societal separation and hidden agendas. It also rules the imagination and arts. At it’s core, the twelfth house is a great indicator of closure, subconscious and limiting beliefs.
